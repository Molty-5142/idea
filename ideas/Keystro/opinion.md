# Keystro — Opinion (Updated: Pivot 1 — Context from Typing)

## The Pivot in Plain Terms

The original critique was: "iOS sandbox blocks cross-app context reading." That was a real concern.

The reframe: **the keyboard doesn't need to read other apps**. Every keystroke goes through the keyboard extension. That IS the context. Type "following up on the showing at" → AI knows you're a RE agent doing a follow-up → completes the sentence. No screen reading. No cross-app access. Clean.

This resolves the original technical kill risk. But does it make the product significantly better?

**Mostly yes. One new risk emerged.**

---

## Updated Assessment

### What Got Better

**The value proposition is now simpler and cleaner:**
- Old: "AI keyboard that reads your context across apps"
- New: "AI keyboard that completes your sentences and suggests replies as you type"

This is easier to explain, easier to trust, and technically straightforward. It's essentially Copilot for mobile texting — which is a real product category gap.

**The iOS full access trust barrier is more manageable for professionals:**
- Gboard has full access. Professionals who use Grammarly have granted similar permissions.
- For RE agents specifically, this is a business tool decision, not a personal privacy one.
- They already trust tools like Follow Up Boss with far more sensitive data (client records, commission info).

**The competitive gap vs. TextExpander is still real:**
- TextExpander launched AI in 2025, but it's snippet-suggestion focused and desktop-dominant.
- Mobile texting — iMessage, WhatsApp, Instagram DMs — is where agents actually close deals.
- TextExpander AI does not help an agent craft a fresh reply from scratch in iMessage. Keystro does.

### What Got Worse (or Remained Bad)

**TextExpander AI is now a threat — it wasn't before.**
- They've added "suggest snippet based on context" and "create snippets on the fly" features.
- They already have RE agent trust, B2B sales motion, and enterprise relationships.
- If they add mobile keyboard depth, they could squeeze Keystro from above.
- Probability they go mobile-first: Low (they're desktop DNA). But watch this.

**Compose AI validates the model — and shows the desktop is already served.**
- Compose AI (Chrome extension) does context-from-typing autocomplete on desktop.
- Mobile is the gap. The question is: does the gap justify a standalone keyboard app?

**The teen/consumer persona is still dead.**
- iOS full access trust barrier is effectively a dealbreaker for consumer mass market.
- Rizz app commodity competition remains.
- Don't build for teens. Drop this persona entirely.

---

## Which Persona Is Stronger (Still Real Estate, Clearer Now)

Real estate. No contest. And now we're more certain why:

1. **They text from their phone constantly** — iMessage, WhatsApp, Instagram DMs. This is where the gap is.
2. **The AI value is real without screen reading** — "I'm a RE agent in Phoenix following up on a showing" stored in persona + typing context = smart completions. No black magic required.
3. **TextExpander AI doesn't touch mobile texting** — their moat is desktop. Mobile is open.
4. **Verse.ai, Smarter Contact, Ylopo are all automation tools** — not real-time agent assistance. Different category.

---

## Is the AI Advantage Real Without Screen Reading?

**Yes, but it's thinner than the original pitch implied.**

With screen reading, you'd get: "The user is in iMessage, texting someone named Sarah who was a lead from 6 months ago."
Without screen reading, you get: "The user is typing a follow-up message about a showing."

That's still useful. The persona config (I'm a RE agent in Phoenix, here are my common clients) + what they're typing is enough for 80% of completions. But it's not as magical as the original vision.

The moat becomes:
- **Persona memory** — the keyboard knows your role, your market, your common phrases
- **RE domain training** — suggestions sound like a RE agent, not like ChatGPT
- **Speed** — in-keyboard = zero friction vs. copy-paste from ChatGPT

## Verdict: NOT APPROVED (7/10 average, need 9+ on each or overall clear)

### Why Not Approved
The pivot resolves the technical sandbox concern but reveals a thinner moat. The product is:
- Real (pain exists)
- Buildable (context-from-typing is proven)
- Monetizable (RE agents pay for tools)
- But not clearly differentiated enough from: (1) TextExpander AI expanding, (2) "just use ChatGPT" workflow, (3) CRM-native tools building messaging features

### What Would Make This Approvable
1. **Proprietary RE training data** — if you had 10,000 agent conversations labeled with outcome (did the deal close?), you could fine-tune for "replies that convert" — that's defensible
2. **CRM integration that actually reads contact context** — even without screen reading, if the keyboard knows the Salesforce record of who you're texting, that's a moat
3. **Brokerage distribution locked** — 5 signed brokerage LOIs before building. That changes the risk profile.

Currently, those aren't in hand.
