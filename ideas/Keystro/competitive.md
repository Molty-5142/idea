# Keystro — Competitive Landscape

## Tier 1: Generic AI Keyboards (Biggest Incumbents)

### Gboard (Google)
- **Price:** Free
- **What it does:** Predictive text, GIF search, voice typing, Google Translate
- **What it can't do:** Context-aware suggestions, tone matching, professional persona switching, domain-specific vocabulary (real estate), reply generation
- **Why the gap exists:** Google optimizes for general-purpose. No incentive to build vertical depth.

### SwiftKey (Microsoft)
- **Price:** Free
- **What it does:** Adaptive prediction, multilingual, theme customization
- **What it can't do:** Same as Gboard — no AI reply generation, no tone context, no vertical specialization
- **Why the gap exists:** Microsoft is focused on Azure AI and Copilot; mobile keyboard is a distribution tool, not a product priority.

### Grammarly Keyboard
- **Price:** Free basic / ~$12–$30/mo premium
- **What it does:** Grammar correction, tone detection, word suggestions
- **What it can't do:** Generate full reply suggestions, match vibe of a conversation, work as a real estate tool, understand MLS jargon or lead psychology
- **Why the gap exists:** Grammarly is a writing assistant, not a response generator. Different use case.

## Tier 2: AI Reply / Rizz Apps (Direct Consumer Competitors)

### Rizz! (iOS, Product Hunt Jan 2023)
- **Price:** ~$4/mo
- **What it does:** AI-powered replies for dating, social, email; keyboard layer
- **What it can't do:** Real estate context, professional tone switching, CRM awareness, team features
- **Gap:** Consumer-only, thin moat, no vertical depth

### CharmKey / RizzMode / RizzKey / WittyKeys
- **Price:** Freemium (in-app purchases)
- **What they do:** AI-suggested flirty/charming replies; emoji suggestions
- **What they can't do:** Any professional context, real world B2B use, domain specialization
- **Gap:** Commodity. Multiple apps fighting for the same novelty market.

### WittyKeys (Google Play)
- **Price:** Freemium
- **What it does:** "Rizz up your chats" — AI keyboard for flirty replies, grammar, translation
- **Same gap as above**

## Tier 3: Voice Keyboard Startup

### Willow (YC S25, iOS)
- **Raised:** $4.5M (Box Group, YC, Burst Capital)
- **What it does:** Voice-first keyboard — speak anywhere you type on iPhone, AI formats to match tone/context of the app; 100+ languages; custom team vocabulary; enterprise features (Uber, Heidi Health, Zego)
- **What it can't do:** Text reply generation (you have to speak first); no real estate-specific domain depth; no suggestion of what to say without voice prompt
- **Why the gap exists:** Willow solves "typing is too slow" (voice → text). Keystro solves "I don't know what to say." Different problem, different user moment.
- **Threat level:** MEDIUM-LOW. Different modality. But if Willow adds "suggest a reply" from typing context, they become direct. Watch if they move from voice-input to text-generation.
- **Updated note (Mar 2026):** Growing 50% MoM. Well-funded. If they pivot toward suggestion-based AI (not just dictation), Keystro loses. Currently they haven't.

## Tier 4: Real Estate Communication Tools

### TextExpander (+ TextExpander AI, 2025)
- **Price:** $3.33/mo (individual), $8.33/user/mo (business)
- **What it does (original):** Static text snippet expansion — type abbreviation, expands to full phrase
- **What it does (2025 AI update):** Suggests the right snippet based on context around cursor; creates snippets on the fly; AI drafting within snippet library
- **What it STILL can't do:** Mobile keyboard-layer AI suggestions; help an agent craft a fresh reply in iMessage from scratch; works in every app across mobile
- **Gap (updated):** TextExpander AI has narrowed the desktop gap but mobile texting (iMessage, WhatsApp, Instagram DMs) is still unserved. That's where RE agents actually talk to leads.
- **Threat level:** MEDIUM and rising. They have the customer, the trust, the B2B motion. If they ship a real mobile keyboard, the RE agent vertical becomes contested.
- **Key weakness:** TextExpander is desktop DNA. Mobile is secondary. Their AI is snippet-centric, not reply-generative.

### Follow Up Boss (texting feature)
- **Price:** $49–$500+/mo (full CRM)
- **What it does:** CRM-integrated texting, lead tracking, drip campaigns
- **What it can't do:** Keyboard-layer AI; works only inside their app; no cross-app intelligence
- **Gap:** Locked inside one app. Keystro works everywhere.

### SimpleTexting / Emitrr
- **Price:** $20–$100+/mo
- **What they do:** Bulk SMS, drip campaigns, lead automation
- **What they can't do:** Real-time keyboard-level suggestions; agent-in-the-moment help
- **Gap:** Automation vs. augmentation. Different value prop.

## New Entrants in RE Texting (2025)
- **Smarter Contact** — AI text marketing platform for RE; bulk campaigns, not in-the-moment keyboard help
- **Launch Control** — text messaging for RE wholesalers; automation-focused
- **Verse.ai** — fully-managed AI SMS (bot responds 24/7); not agent-assisting
- **Ylopo** — AI voice + text for lead nurturing; CRM-level, not keyboard-level
- **Compose AI (Chrome extension)** — context-from-typing autocomplete on desktop; validates the model but desktop-only
- **Key pattern**: All 2025 entrants are CRM/automation layer, not keyboard layer. The mobile, in-the-moment, agent-assisting gap remains open.

## The Gap Summary (Updated Mar 2026)

**No product exists that:**
1. Works as a mobile system-level keyboard (works in every app — iMessage, WhatsApp, Instagram, Gmail)
2. Generates context-aware AI reply suggestions from typing context alone (no screen reading)
3. Is built specifically for real estate agents' vocabulary and workflow
4. Gets smarter with a specific agent's voice over time via persona config
5. Integrates with real estate CRMs for true contextual awareness

**What has changed:**
- TextExpander AI (2025) closes the desktop gap — but mobile is still open
- Willow is voice-only — different modality, different problem
- RE automation tools (Verse, Smarter Contact, Ylopo) are bot/CRM layer, not agent assistance

**The consumer space (teen rizz keyboards) is dead for a startup.** iOS trust barrier + commodity competition = don't build for teens.

**The RE professional mobile keyboard gap remains real but the moat is thinner than pre-TextExpander-AI.** Keystro's defensibility depends on mobile-first execution and domain depth before TextExpander goes mobile.
