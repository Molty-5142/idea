# PolicyPulse — Competitive Landscape

---

## Direct Competitors

### 1. Mineral (formerly ThinkHR)
- **What it does:** HR compliance resources, training, policy templates, HR hotline
- **Pricing:** ~$3-5/user/month (sold through brokers/PEOs — NOT direct-to-SMB)
- **Gap:** Not self-serve. You buy it through a broker who marks it up. Hard to even find pricing online.
- **Gap:** No AI-native proactive alerting. It's a document library + hotline, not an intelligent monitor.
- **Gap:** No state-specific personalization based on your actual business profile.
- Source: https://www.itqlick.com/thinkhr-learn/pricing / https://trustmineral.com/

### 2. GovDocs
- **What it does:** Labor law poster updates, multi-location compliance for large employers
- **Pricing:** Enterprise, not listed publicly
- **Gap:** Built for HR departments at 100+ employee companies. Not self-serve for SMBs.
- **Gap:** Focuses on physical labor law posters (compliance theater) — not actual policy drafting.
- Source: https://www.govdocs.com/

### 3. Rippling / Paychex / ADP
- **What it does:** Full HRIS — payroll, benefits, compliance baked in at the system level
- **Pricing:** $40-100+/month (and rising with add-ons)
- **Gap:** You're buying a $70/month payroll system to get compliance. Overkill for existing Gusto/QuickBooks users.
- **Gap:** Compliance is passive (no alerts, no plain-English policy drafting), not AI-native.

### 4. Harvey AI / CoCounsel / Spellbook
- **What it does:** AI for lawyers — contract review, research, drafting
- **Pricing:** Harvey $1,000+/month; CoCounsel from $50/month; Spellbook $199/month
- **Gap:** Designed for attorneys, not business owners. Requires legal literacy to use.
- **Gap:** Not employment-law-specific monitoring. General legal AI.
- Source: https://www.counselpro.ai/blog/best-harvey-ai-alternatives-for-lawyers

### 5. Clio Grow / Lawmatics
- **What it does:** Law firm intake CRM
- **Pricing:** $49-99/month
- **Gap:** Completely different category — for law firms managing clients, not SMBs tracking employment law.

## Indirect Competitors

| Tool | Who It's For | What It Does | Why It's Not PolicyPulse |
|------|-------------|--------------|-------------------------|
| ChatGPT (generic) | Everyone | Answers questions | No state-specific monitoring, no alerts, no policy drafting, no personalization |
| Google / Law firm blogs | DIYers | Static information | Stale, requires user to know what to search for |
| LegalZoom / Rocket Lawyer | Document creation | Template-based contracts | One-time, no ongoing monitoring, no alerts |
| HR attorney on retainer | Well-funded businesses | Reactive legal advice | $2,000-5,000/month, not scalable for 10-person shops |

---

## The Gap PolicyPulse Fills

No one exists that:
- Is self-serve, direct-to-SMB
- Costs $50-150/month (impulse-buy tier)
- Asks 5 questions about your business and delivers a **personalized** compliance feed
- Proactively alerts when laws change in YOUR states
- Drafts updated policy language (not just says "the law changed")
- Is AI-native (not a template library with a chatbot tacked on)

---

## Moat Building

- **Data advantage:** First mover to build a comprehensive, parsed, structured database of state employment law changes → becomes the industry-standard feed
- **Integration moat:** Deep Gusto/Rippling integrations mean switching = losing business-specific data
- **Trust moat:** Once SMBs rely on it for compliance, they don't cancel (compliance is ongoing, not one-shot)
- **Reseller moat:** Once 50+ fractional HR consultants are reselling it, distribution is locked in

---

## Competitive Positioning

> "Mineral is a library. PolicyPulse is a watchdog."

Mineral gives you a document when you ask. PolicyPulse tells you what changed last month, whether it applies to your 18-person restaurant in Colorado, and hands you updated policy language — before your employee files a complaint.
