# PolicyPulse — Marketing & Distribution

---

## Primary Distribution Channels

### 1. Reddit (Organic + Community)
- Post in r/smallbusiness, r/EntrepreneurRideAlong, r/legaladvice with genuinely helpful comments
- When someone posts about HR compliance confusion, answer helpfully then link to PolicyPulse
- Run an "ask me anything about your state's new employment laws" thread
- Cost: $0, requires 2-3 hrs/week

### 2. YouTube SEO + Short-Form Video
- Target: "California minimum wage 2025 small business" / "Oregon retirement mandate employers" / "what is the exempt salary threshold 2025"
- These are high commercial-intent searches (business owners researching compliance)
- Short explainer videos (2-3 min) that naturally promote PolicyPulse as the "set it and forget it" version
- Cost: $0-500/month

### 3. SEO Landing Pages (Programmatic)
- One page per state × law type: "California overtime exemption rules 2025" / "New York paid leave 2026 small business"
- These rank because they're specific and business owners Google them
- Each page ends with CTA: "Don't track this manually — let PolicyPulse alert you"
- 50 states × 10 law types = 500 landing pages
- Cost: $0 (content), slight SEO lift over time

### 4. Gusto / QuickBooks App Store
- List PolicyPulse as a compliance layer on top of payroll
- Business owners already using Gusto are exactly the target — 5-50 employees, set up for digital tools
- Deep integration: PolicyPulse reads employee count + states from Gusto API, pre-fills onboarding
- Cost: app store listing fee typically $0-1000/yr

### 5. HR Consultant / Fractional HR Partnerships
- "Fractional HR" is a growing category — independent HR consultants serving SMBs
- White-label PolicyPulse: HR consultant charges $299/mo to client, pays $99/mo wholesale
- These are walking distribution channels — each one brings 5-20 clients
- Target: LinkedIn outreach to "fractional HR" consultants
- Cost: partnership program, revenue share

### 6. Small Business Facebook Groups
- Massive, underserved. Groups like "Small Business Owners - USA" have 500K+ members
- Post: "Just found out California raised the exempt salary threshold to $68,640 — are you compliant?"
- Follow up with link
- Cost: $0

### 7. Paid: Google Search Ads
- Keyword targets: "employment law compliance tool" / "small business HR compliance" / "wage and hour compliance software"
- Intent is extremely high — someone searching this is already feeling the pain
- Budget: $1,000-3,000/month to test, target $30-50 CAC
- Cost: $1-3K/month early

### 8. Newsletter / Sponsorships
- Sponsor small business newsletters: Morning Brew Small Biz edition, Justin Welsh newsletter, The Hustle
- One-off sponsorships to drive trial
- Cost: $500-2000/issue

---

## Retention Marketing

- **Monthly Law Change Digest**: Email showing what changed in their state(s) this month — high-value, high-open rate
- **"Compliance Score"**: Gamified dashboard showing how compliant your business is — drives engagement
- **Annual Policy Audit**: Auto-generated report of what was updated this year → forward to accountant / board
- **Alert System**: "You need to act on this by March 31st" — creates urgency, reduces churn

---

## Launch Strategy

1. Week 1: Post in r/smallbusiness with genuine compliance help thread. Collect 50 emails.
2. Week 2: Publish 5 SEO blog posts targeting high-intent state-specific keywords.
3. Week 3: Cold email 50 fractional HR consultants on LinkedIn with white-label offer.
4. Week 4: Launch Product Hunt. Angle: "Your AI that reads employment law so you don't have to."
5. Month 2: Activate Gusto App Store listing.
6. Month 3: First paid Google Search experiment ($1K budget).
