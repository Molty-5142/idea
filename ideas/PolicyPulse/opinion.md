# PolicyPulse — Opinion / Analysis

---

## The Angle I'm Betting On

**Persona:** Small business owner with 5-50 employees — restaurant, retail, construction, services.  
**Pain:** Every 6 months, some state law changes (minimum wage, salary threshold, paid leave, background check rules). They find out because an employee sues them or an auditor shows up.  
**Gap:** Existing tools (Mineral, GovDocs) are sold through HR brokers to mid-market. Direct-to-SMB, self-serve, affordable, plain-English AI doesn't exist.

---

## Why This Is Real

The Reddit post from January 2026 nails it perfectly: *"half the time I don't even know if it applies to me or not."* This is the core of the pain — not ignorance, not laziness. It's the impossibility of tracking 50 states × multiple laws × multiple update cycles × their specific business situation. No human can do this manually.

The California example is damning: minimum exempt salary went to $68,640 in 2025. That's a new threshold. Businesses that didn't update classifications now face misclassification claims. This happens every year, in almost every state.

---

## Why AI Is 10x Better Here

A spreadsheet, template, or checklist CAN'T solve this:
- Laws change. Static templates go stale.
- Applicability depends on: number of employees, location (city/county/state), industry, job type.
- A checklist doesn't know your specific payroll setup.

AI is uniquely suited:
- Continuously scrape and parse legislation changes from state labor department feeds
- Ask 5 onboarding questions (state, headcount, industry, pay types, contractor usage)
- Generate a personalized compliance snapshot + updated policy language
- Alert when something changes that applies specifically to THEM

This is not a GPT wrapper. The value is in: data pipeline (law changes) + personalization engine (what applies to you) + auto-drafting (updated policies).

---

## The Business I Would Build

**MVP:** 
1. Onboarding: 5-question form (state, size, industry, pay structure, contractor use)
2. Monthly digest: "Here are 3 law changes this month that affect your business"
3. One-click policy update: "Here's your updated overtime policy (ready to sign)"

**V2:**
- Expansion to multi-state (for remote-first SMBs — huge TAM expansion)
- Integration with Gusto, Rippling, ADP — push policy updates directly
- White-label for HR consultants / PEOs to resell

---

## Risks

1. **Trust barrier:** Will small business owners trust AI with compliance? Yes — they already Google this stuff and take ChatGPT's word for it. This is just structured and reliable.
2. **Legal liability:** Need strong disclaimers. Not a law firm. But Mineral does this fine.
3. **Content freshness:** Need a law change pipeline. This is the hard part. Could start manually curating for top 5-10 states, automate later.
4. **Competitor response:** Mineral could launch a self-serve tier. But they're broker-distribution focused and won't move fast.

---

## Why This Isn't a KILL

- Pain is proven and acute (real quotes, real lawsuits)
- AI advantage is genuine and not replicable by checklist/template
- Market is enormous (34M small businesses, even 0.1% TAM = 34,000 customers)
- No direct AI-native, self-serve SMB competitor exists at sub-$150/month
- Pricing math works cleanly (see pitch.md)
