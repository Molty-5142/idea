# SwapIQ — Opinion & Analysis

## What's Actually Happening

1031 investors aren't buying bad properties because they're dumb. They're buying bad properties because **45 days is not enough time to do proper due diligence manually** across multiple replacement candidates, especially when they're also managing a closing, coordinating with a QI, and managing their existing portfolio.

The current workflow is: close on your relinquished property → panic-search LoopNet → try to remember how to build a spreadsheet → hope your broker sends you something decent → identify 3 properties before day 45 → pray.

There's no tool that:
1. Starts the 45-day countdown and nudges you
2. Helps you filter MLS/LoopNet/Crexi for like-kind compliance
3. Automatically pulls in and parses an OM or rent roll
4. Runs basic underwriting (cap rate, NOI, boot calculation, equity requirement)
5. Ranks your top 3 candidates and highlights risks

## Why AI Is Essential Here (Not Just Automation)

The core challenge is **document-heavy reasoning under deadline**:
- OM docs are 20–80 pages, unstructured, require understanding CAP rate context, market assumptions
- Like-kind rules require reasoning ("is a gas station like-kind to a strip mall?")
- Boot calculations require understanding stepped basis from the relinquished property
- Risk flagging requires judgment: tenant concentration, lease expiry timing, market conditions

This isn't automatable with simple rules. It needs LLM-grade document understanding and reasoning.

## Why This Market Is Winnable

- No VC has piled in: there's no "Dealpath for 1031 investors" despite the obvious gap
- QIs (qualified intermediaries) are highly motivated to add value — could be a distribution channel
- Per-transaction pricing is natural and defensible (tied to real economic events)
- The investor has maximum willingness to pay RIGHT NOW: they're looking at a potential $50K–$500K tax bill if the exchange fails
- Switching cost is nil — investors do maybe 1-5 exchanges per year, so stickiness is via reputation not lock-in

## Key Risks

1. **Market size ceiling**: ~100K exchanges/year, not all active investors (many use DSTs/passive). True active investor TAM might be 20–40K/year.
2. **Data access**: Need LoopNet, Crexi, or CoStar integration — API access can be expensive
3. **Liability**: Can't give tax advice; must position as "deal analysis tool, not tax counsel"
4. **One-shot usage pattern**: Per-exchange pricing means repeat purchases aren't guaranteed without newsletter/community

## Verdict

This is a **sharp, acute, proven pain** with no AI-native solution. The 45-day deadline creates urgency that bypasses the usual "I'll evaluate this software later" buyer behavior. Per-transaction pricing aligns with value. Market size is modest (~$10M ARR ceiling realistic), but path to $500K ARR is clear and achievable at low acquisition costs.

**Score estimate: 8.5/10** — Needs the critic's eye on market ceiling.
