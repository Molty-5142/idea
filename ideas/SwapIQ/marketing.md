# SwapIQ — Marketing & Distribution

## Core Distribution Insight

The 1031 investor is **event-triggered**. They don't browse SaaS tools in downtime — they panic-search when their exchange clock starts. The best marketing targets:
1. The moment before they sell (tax planning content)
2. The moment they close (QI referral at the highest-urgency moment)

## Channel Strategy

### 1. QI Partnerships (Highest Leverage)
- Qualified intermediaries (IPX1031, Equity Advantage, 1031x, etc.) touch every exchange
- They hold the funds but don't help investors find properties — perfect referral fit
- Pitch: "Offer SwapIQ to your clients as a free/discounted add-on → your clients complete exchanges → you make more money"
- Revenue share with QIs: 20% per activated paid exchange
- ~100 large QI firms + hundreds of smaller ones

### 2. CPA / Tax Advisor Network
- CPAs recommend 1031 to clients during tax planning season (Q1–Q4, but especially Sep–Dec)
- They want to look smart by pointing clients to useful tools
- Outreach: email list of CPAs who focus on RE investors (find via AICPA, state CPA societies)
- Offer: "White-label affiliate link, 20% cut of each completed exchange"

### 3. SEO — High-Intent Long-Tail
- "1031 exchange replacement property deadline tool"
- "how to find replacement property 1031 exchange 45 days"
- "1031 exchange analyzer"
- "1031 exchange calculator replacement property"
- Write the definitive guide: "How to Analyze a 1031 Replacement Property in 45 Days"

### 4. Real Estate Investor Communities
- BiggerPockets: massive investor community, allow vendor posts in certain forums
- r/realestateinvesting: engage authentically (post the guide, not ads)
- Real estate investor Facebook groups (search "1031 exchange investors")
- Local REIA (Real Estate Investor Association) chapters — present the tool at meetings

### 5. Podcast Sponsorships
- BiggerPockets Podcast (top RE investing podcast, millions of listeners)
- Real Estate Guys Radio
- Passive Pockets
- Cost per read: ~$500–$2,000/episode; CPM justified given high-value audience

### 6. Retargeting
- Pixel anyone who reads your "1031 deadline guide" content
- Retarget with "Did you close on your sale? Your 45-day clock may already be running."

## Hook/Angle

The best hook: **Fear of a blown exchange**

"The average investor pays $73,000 in capital gains taxes when their 1031 exchange fails. Not because they didn't try — because 45 days wasn't enough time to do it right."

This hits immediately because:
1. It's true (calculable)
2. It's specific
3. It creates urgency at the exact moment of need
