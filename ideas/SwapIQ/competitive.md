# SwapIQ — Competitive Landscape

## Direct Competitors (Replacement Property Screening)

**None found.** No AI-native tool specifically designed to help 1031 investors screen and analyze replacement properties under the 45-day deadline.

## Indirect Competitors / Adjacent Tools

### 1031 Crowdfunding / Passive DST Platforms
- **1031 Crowdfunding** (1031crowdfunding.com): Curates passive DST investments only. Not for active investors who want to buy their own properties. Target: passive investor who wants to defer and check out.
- **RealtyMogul, Crowdstreet**: Same — DST/syndication plays, not active property analysis

### Property Analysis Tools (Generic)
- **DealCheck** ($10–$20/mo): General rental property analyzer. No 1031 awareness, no deadline, no like-kind compliance, no OM parsing.
- **PropertyMetrics**: CRE financial modeling, spreadsheet-oriented, no 1031 workflow
- **FlipperForce**: Fix-and-flip focused, not 1031

### CRE Listing Platforms
- **LoopNet / CoStar**: Listings only, no analysis layer. No 1031 filtering.
- **Crexi**: Better UI, still listings only. Has some analysis features but not 1031-specific.

### QI Firms with Digital Tools
- **IPX1031**: Largest QI. Digital portal for fund management, not property screening.
- **1031x, Equity Advantage**: Basic informational sites. No software tools.

### AI Legal/CRE Platforms
- **Orbital Copilot**: AI for CRE attorneys doing title/survey review. Not investor-facing.
- **Leasey.AI**: Lease management and PM software. Not acquisition/exchange workflow.

## Competitive Gap

The gap is clear:
- QIs handle compliance and funds but not property finding/analysis
- Listing platforms have inventory but no exchange-specific workflow
- Generic deal analyzers don't understand 1031 constraints (boot, basis, like-kind rules)
- No tool combines: deadline tracking + property search + document parsing + underwriting + ranking

## Why the Gap Persists

1. Market is perceived as "small" by VCs (100K exchanges/year vs. millions of rentals)
2. 1031 is complex — builders fear the legal/tax liability exposure
3. The QI industry is relationship-driven and slow to adopt technology
4. Most investors do this once every few years, so the pain feels episodic

## Moat Potential

- First-mover in a specific enough niche that VC tools ignore
- QI partnership network creates defensible distribution
- Proprietary underwriting logic tuned to 1031 rules
- Community/brand: investors who went through an exchange remember what helped them
