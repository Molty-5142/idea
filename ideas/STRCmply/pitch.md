# pitch.md — STR Comply: Investor One-Pager

## Problem
Short-term rental hosts are getting blindsided by fines of $5,000–$20,000+ for unknowingly violating local regulations that change constantly — while cities arm themselves with AI enforcement tools to catch them, and hosts have nothing to defend themselves with.

## Solution
STR Comply is an AI-powered compliance co-pilot for Airbnb/VRBO hosts that monitors your local regulations, tracks permit deadlines, and alerts you before you get fined — so you can focus on hosting, not bureaucracy.

---

## Vision
Become the compliance layer for the $20B short-term rental industry — embedded in every property management workflow as the trust infrastructure that keeps hosts legal, operating, and profitable.

Long-term: expand into 1031 exchange coordination, rental property tax prep, and broader real estate regulatory intelligence. The short-term rental host is the gateway customer to a broader real estate operator persona.

---

## Market Size

- **5 million** Airbnb hosts globally; ~1.4 million in the US
- **400+ US cities** now require STR permits/licenses, growing by ~50/year
- **Serviceable market:** ~500,000 US hosts who operate in regulated jurisdictions and generate meaningful income (>$20K/year from STR)
- **TAM:** $3B+ (500K hosts × $500/year average blended ARPU × broad regulatory expansion)
- **SAM (Year 1–3):** Top 10 STR markets in the US = ~150,000 hosts = $45M ARR potential

---

## Why Now

1. **Enforcement is accelerating.** Portland's fines are 27x comparable cities. NYC effectively banned most STRs. New Orleans mass-delisted hosts in summer 2025. Governments are now *using AI to catch hosts.*
2. **Regulatory velocity is increasing.** States filing new STR bills in 2025–2026. Every quarter brings new ordinances.
3. **No host-side solution exists.** All compliance tools sell to governments. The host-facing gap is real and uncontested.
4. **AI makes it possible.** Scraping, parsing, and synthesizing 400+ city ordinances + monitoring changes is an AI-native problem. Not possible with humans at scale.
