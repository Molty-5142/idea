# marketing.md — STR Comply: How to Reach Users

## Target User
- Airbnb/VRBO hosts with 1–20 properties (prosumer to small portfolio)
- Specifically: hosts who take their operation seriously and want to stay legal
- Secondary: new hosts who just listed their first property and don't know what they don't know

## Channel 1: Reddit Organic (Free, High Intent)
**r/airbnb_hosts (700K+ members), r/ShortTermRentals, r/realestateinvesting**

Tactic: Answer compliance questions as a subject matter expert. When someone asks "do I need a permit in Denver?" — give a thorough answer and mention the tool as a resource. Not spam, genuine help.

Bonus: Create a weekly "STR Compliance Digest" post per city subreddit. Builds brand as the compliance authority.

## Channel 2: BiggerPockets Community (High Intent Investors)
BiggerPockets forums have dedicated STR threads. 2M+ members. Compliance questions come up constantly. Write the definitive STR compliance guide for each major city and post it. Drive traffic via organic search from these posts too.

## Channel 3: "Free City Check" Lead Magnet
Build a free "Is Your STR Legally Compliant?" checker for the 10 largest STR markets. User enters city + address, gets a compliance checklist for that jurisdiction. Email gated. High conversion because it solves an immediate question.

This is the top-of-funnel machine. Estimated conversion: 15–25% from free check to trial.

## Channel 4: STR Facebook Groups
The Facebook STR host community is enormous:
- "Airbnb Hosts Forum" (~100K members)
- "Vacation Rental Owners and Managers" (~80K members)
- City-specific host groups (Nashville STR Hosts, Denver Airbnb Hosts, etc.)

These groups have constant compliance questions. Direct value-add posting.

## Channel 5: YouTube / Short-Form Video
"What happens if you operate an Airbnb without a permit in [City]?" — search-optimized explainer videos for each major market. Real fine cases (Portland's $20K story is perfect content). Drives brand awareness and organic search traffic.

Titles that work:
- "Portland fined this Airbnb host $20,000 — here's how to avoid it"
- "The 5 permits you need before your first Airbnb guest (in Austin)"
- "NYC just banned most Airbnbs — is yours legal?"

## Channel 6: Hostaway / Hospitable Partner Integration
Approach PMS platforms as a compliance partner. They have 100K+ hosts. A native integration that adds a "Compliance Status" tab to their dashboard is massive distribution at zero CAC.

Hostaway explicitly says: "some PMS platforms also integrate with compliance-specific services." This is a warm door.

## Channel 7: Local STR Host Associations
Many cities have organized STR host advocacy groups (e.g., Nashville STR Alliance, Keep My Home San Diego). These groups desperately need compliance resources. Sponsor their newsletters, host webinars. CAC is near zero.

## Channel 8: LinkedIn / B2B Angle
Target: Real estate property managers moving into STR, vacation rental management companies scaling up. LinkedIn content about regulatory risk management. B2B deal: $200–500/month for portfolio plans.

## Channel 9: Google Ads (Performance-Based)
Keywords: "airbnb permit [city]", "short term rental license [city]", "airbnb regulations [city]"
These have high commercial intent and relatively low CPCs vs. general real estate.
Estimated CPC: $1.50–$4.00
Target: get to $20 CAC at these rates.

## Channel 10: Fine Horror Stories (PR Playbook)
Portland $20K story already got press coverage. Every time a city fines a host, pitch the local press for coverage — mention STR Comply as the solution. This is earned media that compounds over time.
