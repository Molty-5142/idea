# competitive.md — STR Comply: Competitive Landscape

## The Core Finding: No Direct Competitors for Host-Side Compliance

After thorough research, **no product currently serves STR hosts with proactive compliance monitoring and alerting.** The market is split between:
1. Government enforcement tools (sell to cities to catch hosts)
2. PMS tools (manage bookings/comms, compliance is an afterthought)

---

## Government-Side Tools (NOT competition — they're actually market validation)

### Deckard Technologies / Rentalscape
- **Who buys it:** Local governments
- **What it does:** Monitors 15M+ listings, identifies non-compliant STRs, sends enforcement data to cities
- **Price:** B2G contracts, not public pricing
- **Relevance:** This is the hammer; STR Comply is the shield for hosts
- URL: https://deckard.com/short-term-rental

### Host Compliance (Acquired by Granicus)
- **Who buys it:** Municipalities
- **What it does:** Compliance monitoring + enforcement for governments
- **Price:** Enterprise B2G
- **Relevance:** Same as Deckard — sells the prosecution, not the defense
- URL: https://hostcompliance.com/features

### Avenu Insights
- **Who buys it:** Local governments
- **What it does:** STR revenue recovery for municipalities
- **Relevance:** B2G only
- URL: https://www.avenuinsights.com/

---

## Property Management Systems (PMS) — Adjacent, Not Solving This

### Hostaway
- **Price:** From ~$100/month
- **Compliance approach:** General guidance blog posts; says hosts should "integrate compliance-specific services"
- **Gap:** No regulatory monitoring, no permit deadline alerts, no per-jurisdiction rule tracking
- **Opportunity:** Integration partner potential

### Hospitable
- **Price:** From ~$29/month
- **Compliance approach:** None — booking/messaging automation only
- **Gap:** Zero compliance features

### Hostfully / Lodgify / iGMS
- All focused on channel management, messaging, dynamic pricing
- None have meaningful compliance features
- None monitor regulation changes

---

## Closest Adjacent Products

### The Host Report (thehostreport.com)
- Weekly newsletter covering STR regulation updates
- **Price:** Free newsletter
- **Weakness:** Manual, weekly cadence, not personalized to your city/property, no alerts, no action items
- **Gap:** Information without action — you read it but don't know what to do
- URL: https://www.thehostreport.com/

### Awning.com (awning.com/regulations)
- Static info pages about city regulations
- **Price:** Free static content
- **Weakness:** Doesn't alert you when rules change, not actionable, no permit tracking
- **Gap:** Point-in-time snapshot, not ongoing monitoring

### Pricelabs
- Dynamic pricing for STRs
- **Relevance:** None for compliance — different category entirely

---

## Human Alternatives (What Hosts Currently Do)

1. **Hire a local STR compliance consultant** — $500–$2,000/engagement, one-time, doesn't monitor changes
2. **Call city hall and hope for the right answer** (the Portland $20K case — a city staffer gave wrong permit advice)
3. **Ask Reddit** — free but unreliable, no accountability
4. **Just wing it** — most hosts don't know what they don't know (per AMA: "9 out of 10 STRs in our area are not permitted")

---

## Why the Gap Persists

1. **Regulatory data is fragmented.** 400+ cities, all with different rules, stored in PDFs, city websites, ordinance databases. Hard to aggregate.
2. **Market recently became enforcement-heavy.** Before 2023–2024, cities mostly looked the other way. Now enforcement is ramping rapidly — the need just became acute.
3. **B2G pays better.** The obvious business case was selling to cities (six-figure contracts). No one has flipped the model to serve hosts.

---

## Defensibility

- **Data moat:** The city regulation database is hard and expensive to build. First mover wins.
- **Trust moat:** Hosts will trust the compliance tool that gets them out of trouble. Once trusted, sticky.
- **Integration moat:** PMS integrations create lock-in through workflow embedding.
