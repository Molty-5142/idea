# IncomeIQ — Investor One-Pager

---

## The Problem

Every mortgage loan officer dreads the same 90-minute trap: a self-employed borrower calls, seems solid, the LO spends an hour collecting tax returns and manually working through the Fannie Mae 1084 worksheet — only to discover the borrower wrote off too much to qualify. No loan. No pay. Time gone.

With 36 million self-employed Americans trying to buy homes, and self-employed mortgages representing ~15-18% of all applications, this happens thousands of times a day nationwide.

The trigger lead ban (effective March 5, 2026) makes each inbound lead MORE valuable — loan officers can no longer cheaply buy pipeline. Wasting time on non-qualifying borrowers now has a higher real cost.

**Expert LOs screen verbally in 10 minutes. Everyone else flies blind.**

---

## The Solution

**IncomeIQ** is an AI-powered income pre-screener for mortgage loan officers. Before collecting a single document, the LO walks through a structured conversational intake with the borrower. IncomeIQ:

- Estimates qualifying income range from verbal inputs alone
- Recommends the right loan pathway (conventional 1084, bank statement, DSCR, non-QM, no-go)
- Flags likely qualification issues before doc collection
- Surfaces fix-it paths ("delay 1 year," "restructure write-offs," "try bank statement instead")
- Takes 8-12 minutes vs. 60-90 minutes for full analysis

This is not automation — it's AI reasoning applied to a context-heavy, exception-filled problem that no spreadsheet or simple calculator can solve.

## Risks

1. **Accuracy**: Wrong pre-screens erode trust. Mitigated by: explicit "pre-screen, not commitment" framing, confidence intervals, human override
2. **Guideline staleness**: Mortgage guidelines change quarterly. Mitigated by: standing subscription to Fannie/Freddie/FHA/VA guideline updates + weekly review process
3. **Competition**: Zeitro or Rapidio could expand scope. Mitigated by: move fast, build brand trust, lock in NAMB/AIME partnership first
