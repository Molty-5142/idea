# IncomeIQ — Opinion & Analysis

---

## The Core Insight

Every loan officer has the same nightmare: they spend 60-90 minutes collecting, organizing, and manually calculating income from a self-employed borrower's tax returns — only to discover the borrower doesn't qualify. That's time they'll never get back, and they won't get paid.

The root cause: **there's no reliable way to pre-screen self-employed borrowers without documents**. The decision tree in a veteran LO's head can take years to build. A newer LO has nothing — they dive in blind.

**IncomeIQ bridges this gap.** Before collecting a single document, an LO answers a structured set of AI-guided questions (business type, years in operation, how they write off expenses, approximate gross revenue vs. net, entity structure, W2 take, rental income, etc.). The AI outputs:

1. Estimated qualifying income range (not a commitment — a pre-screen)
2. Most likely loan pathway (conventional 1084, bank statement, DSCR, FHA, no-go)
3. What documents to request and in what order
4. Flags for likely issues (high write-offs, declining income, 2-year minimum not met)
5. Suggested "fix-it" paths (delay 1 year, restructure write-offs, use bank statements instead)

This isn't replacing underwriting. It's giving an LO the expert gut-check they would get if they had a veteran beside them on every call.

---

## Why AI Wins Here

This is not a simple automation problem. The reason it doesn't exist yet:

- **Context-dependent**: Income calculation rules vary by loan type (conventional/FHA/VA/USDA/non-QM), entity structure (sole prop / LLC / S-corp / partnership), income source (Schedule C / K-1 / Schedule E / rental), and trend (increasing vs declining)
- **Ambiguous inputs**: Borrowers describe their income in plain language ("I made $180k last year but wrote off a lot") — mapping this to qualification math requires reasoning
- **Exception-heavy**: Non-QM guidelines change frequently; what qualifies at one wholesale lender doesn't at another
- **Heuristic-heavy**: Expert LOs use pattern matching built from thousands of scenarios — classic use case for large language model reasoning

This is **genuine AI reasoning**, not just if/then logic. The alternative would be a 50-page decision tree that nobody would use.

---

## What's Real About the Pain

The self-employed borrower population is large and growing:
- 36M self-employed in the US
- Rising gig economy = more 1099 / non-W2 workers trying to buy homes
- Post-trigger-lead ban (March 2026): every inbound lead is MORE valuable, so wasted time with non-qualifying borrowers hurts more

LOs that primarily handle self-employed borrowers (non-QM brokers, wealth LOs) are especially at risk. A 45-minute wasted intake is a real cost.

---

## Risks & Doubts

**1. Accuracy risk**: If IncomeIQ says "likely qualifies" and they don't, LO trust erodes fast. Mitigation: always frame as pre-screen, not commitment. Show confidence intervals ("likely qualifies at $X ± 15%").

**2. Existing experience**: Many veteran LOs already have this intuition. They're not the target. Target is the long tail of 100K+ mid-level LOs and brokers who close 3-8 loans/month and still make occasional calculation errors.

**3. Rapidio competition**: Rapidio does post-document income calculation. IncomeIQ is pre-document screening. They're different tools in the funnel. Could even be complementary.

**4. Willingness to pay**: LOs are famously cost-sensitive — they're commission-only and tools eat margin. Counter: if IncomeIQ saves 1 wasted intake per week (60 min = ~$200 in opportunity cost), $99/month is an obvious win.

---

## The Wedge Strategy

Start narrow: **S-corporation / small business owner scenarios** (Schedule C + 1120S with K-1s). This is consistently the hardest to analyze quickly and the most common complex case. Build confidence there, then expand to all self-employed scenarios, then rental income, then non-QM bank statement qualification.

- 160,000 licensed LOs in the US
- Target: ~80,000 independent brokers and small-to-mid lenders
- Realistic penetration in 3 years: 3% = 2,400 LOs
- ARPU: $99-149/month
- ARR at 3% penetration: $2.4M - $4.3M ARR
- 1% penetration = $720K - $1.1M ARR (hits $500K easily)

Strong real pain, proven by forum complaints and industry inefficiency. AI is the right tool (not just automation). Main concern: Scenario AI by Zeitro launched 2 months ago and could expand into pre-screening. Also Rapidio is growing. Window is open but narrowing.
